I've always designed Codex-Cryptica to make your world-building smarter, faster, and more connected. But I realized that world-building shouldn't stop the moment your session starts.

I am introducing the **Virtual Tabletop (VTT)** — a tactical layer integrated directly into your archive. This isn't a separate app or a subscription service I'm trying to sell you. It is a natural extension of your notes, built to turn your lore into a playable space without ever compromising your privacy.

## Privacy First: No Servers, No Cloud

I believe your campaign data belongs to you, not a cloud provider. I built this VTT from the ground up to be **entirely serverless**.

- **Zero Data on My End:** I don't run a database or a storage server for your worlds. Your maps, tokens, and notes stay in your browser.
- **Direct Privacy:** When you run a session, your data is shared directly with your players. There is no middleman, no "cloud" storage to manage, and zero risk of your private prep being scanned or analyzed by a third party.
- **The Only Exception:** The only "server" involved in Codex-Cryptica is a tiny proxy I run to securely connect your browser to the Gemini API for the Lore Oracle. Your world data itself never touches it.
- **No Storage Limits:** I don't believe in upload quotas. Since the data stays on your machine, you aren't limited by "pro" storage tiers. If your computer can hold the image, you can use it as a map.

## Your Lore is the Map

The core idea behind the Codex-Cryptica VTT is that it actually understands your world.

In most VTTs, a token is just a name and an image. In my implementation, **a token is an Entity**. When you drag an NPC onto the map, they bring their entire history, their relationships, and the insights of the Lore Oracle with them. You don't have to hunt through folders to remember a character's motivation during a tense negotiation—the map is just another view of your world's data.

## Tactical Discovery and Fog of War

A good session is built on mystery and exploration. I've included a lightweight **Fog of War** system that allows you to hide and reveal areas of the map as your players move.

Whether you need a precise square grid for a dungeon crawl or a hex map for overland travel, the VTT provides the tactical clarity you need without the overhead of complex, heavy engines. I've designed it to be fast, responsive, and focused entirely on the story you are telling.

## The VTT is the Interface

I didn't build a VTT to replace your notes; I built it to make your notes more useful.

Whether you are tracking a party's movement across a continent or managing a room-to-room encounter, this integration ensures that the distance between "writing the world" and "playing the world" is zero.

The VTT is currently in **Early Access (Staging)** and will be rolling out to everyone soon. I can’t wait to see the maps you build and the stories you tell.
