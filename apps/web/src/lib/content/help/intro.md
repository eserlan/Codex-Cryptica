## Welcome to Codex Cryptica

<img
  src="https://assets.codexcryptica.com/cdn-cgi/image/format=auto,quality=80/images/help/getting-started/dashboard.jpg"
  alt="Codex Cryptica dashboard showing a local campaign vault"
  title="Codex Cryptica campaign workspace"
/>

Codex Cryptica (CC) is a **local-first, privacy-first tabletop worldbuilding campaign manager**. Your vault is stored locally in your browser-backed private storage, and you can export or sync it when you want ordinary files or backups outside the browser.

You do not need an account or an AI key to create, edit, link, search, map, or organize your vault. The Lore Oracle and other AI tools are optional.

---

## Quick Start: Your First 5 Minutes

Follow this quick checklist to set up your first vault and see Codex Cryptica's core workflow in action:

<img
  src="https://assets.codexcryptica.com/cdn-cgi/image/format=auto,quality=80/images/help/getting-started/first-entity.jpg"
  alt="Creating a first entity in Codex Cryptica"
  title="Create your first entity"
/>

1.  **Initialize your Space**: Click the folder icon in the top-left toolbar, select **New Vault**, and give it a name.
2.  **Create your First Cast Member**: Click the `+` icon in the explorer sidebar, select the **Character** category, type `Eldrin` as the title, and hit Save.
3.  **Write and Link**: Double-click Eldrin in the list to open his profile, click the Chronicle text area to edit, and type:
    `A legendary mage living in [[Kingdom of Aethel]].`
4.  **Confirm the Location**: The name `Kingdom of Aethel` will automatically create a draft entity link. Click that link in read mode to create the new Faction/Location record.
5.  **Open the Knowledge Graph**: Select the Graph tab in the main navigation. You will see Eldrin and the Kingdom of Aethel mapped visually, with a connecting edge automatically drawn!

## What to Create First

If you are starting from an empty campaign, create five entities:

- one important location
- two major characters
- one faction
- one current conflict or mystery

Write one or two sentences for each, then link names as they come up. This gives the graph enough structure to become useful without needing to plan the whole world first.

---

## Core Concepts

- **Vault**: Your campaign workspace. It contains entities, relationships, maps, calendars, and layouts.
- **Entity**: A person, place, faction, event, item, creature, or note.
- **Chronicle**: The main public-facing text for an entity.
- **Lore**: Private GM notes and secrets. Lore is kept separate from player-facing exports.
- **Connections**: Relationships between entities. Connections power the graph and make the vault easier to navigate.

---

## Write and Link

Write in the Chronicle field the same way you would write campaign notes. In read mode, names that match existing entities or aliases are highlighted as clickable links.

For faster relationship work, use `/connect` from the command bar or the graph connection tools. Use `/merge` when you need to combine duplicate entities.

## Useful Views

<img
  src="https://assets.codexcryptica.com/cdn-cgi/image/format=auto,quality=80/images/help/getting-started/graph.jpg"
  alt="Knowledge graph showing connected campaign entities"
  title="Open the graph to see how your lore connects"
/>

Use the activity bar to switch between different ways of looking at the same vault:

- **Graph**: See how people, places, factions, and events connect.
- **Table**: Sort, filter, and scan many entities at once.
- **Timeline / Calendar / Agenda**: Track dated and approximate events when your campaign history gets larger.
- **Canvas**: Arrange notes and entities spatially for planning.
- **Map**: Add maps, pins, tokens, measurements, and map fog-of-war.

In the graph, press `C` to toggle connect mode and `L` to toggle node labels. Mark entities private or GM-only when they should be omitted from player-facing exports.

---

## Optional AI

The Lore Oracle can help brainstorm, revise, summarize, create drafts, and suggest possible connections. Configure a Gemini key in Settings if you want to use AI-backed features. Your core vault remains usable without it.

---

## Back Up and Share

Vaults are local by default, so backups are important. Export or sync important vaults regularly, especially before clearing browser data or moving to a new device.

When you are ready to share with players, use publishing/player snapshot features. Private and GM-only content is kept separate from player-facing exports.
